The Pivot
The Pivot is a strategy used to test how well a defensive line stays in formation after the ball has passed. The idea is to pass the ball all the way down the line to the wing, then the pivot player gives the wing an option to pass it back in the direction it came so that the ball can be passed down the line again in the opposite direction.

The third from last player on the open side is always the "Pivot" player. To initiate they call "Pivot right!" if the right hand side is the open side, or "Pivot left!" if the left hand side is the open side.
To effectively execute the pivot play, the attacking line must be aware they need to get back into an attacking position after passing the ball, so communication is invaluable. So after hearing the Pivot's call, the other's should then echo the call, or call "cancel" if they disagree strongly.

Once play has started, pass the ball down the line to the wing, trying to get the ball away as soon as possible to make sure it makes it. After passing, players should try to get back into a position ready to attack the other direction.

When approaching the end of the line, the "Pivot" must race back to make a deep inside pass back from the wing possible. The player next to the wing will stay up infront of play because they do not have enough time to both give the wing the pass and get back.

The wing should run toward the line on the outside of their opposing player but try not to get too isolated from the supporting pivot player.
The pivot player should call to receive the ball and be ready to run onto it going the opposite direction.

Once the pivot has been passed the ball, they need to work hard to get back across to connect with their line. There could be a few opposition player up forward because they've broken their line. These are the gaps that should be exploited but can also leave the pivot player isolated if they can't make it back to their supporting players.
Once connected, the ball should be passed along the new lines and any gaps in their defense exploited.
