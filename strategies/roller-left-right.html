Roller Left/Right
This play is a simple move at the breakdown to get the "roller" (the player who kicks the ball back between their legs) back into the game and to get past the marker to attack directly behind where the breakdown is set.

In this example breakdown, the "roller" is ready to kick the ball back, the defensive "marker" is opposite them and the "dummy half" is behind in position to take the ball from the ground.
To initiate the play, the dummy half should call to the roller either "Roller left!" or "Roller right!" depending on which direction they'd like the roller to go. The roller should respond to confirm that they've understood, "alright".

Once the dummy half has taken the ball, they should take a step in the opposite direction from what they've told the roller to do. So if they've called "Roller right!", they should go left, and visa versa.
Meanwhile, the roller should step back and toward the direction they were called. They should be trying to give the dummy half an option to pass as soon as possible. If they are too slow, the dummy half could be tagged by the defensive marker.

The dummy half should then make the decision to pass to the roller or to dummy based on whether they think the marker is coming toward them or the roller.
